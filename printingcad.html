3D Printing & CAD Fundamentals

Master the essential concepts and tools for 3D design and printing

What is 3D Printing?

3D printing, also known as additive manufacturing, creates physical objects from digital designs by building them layer by layer.

CAD Software

Computer-Aided Design (CAD) software allows you to create precise 2D drawings and 3D models for manufacturing and 3D printing.

File Formats

STL and OBJ are the most common file formats for 3D printing, containing the surface geometry of 3D objects.

Printing Materials

PLA, ABS, PETG, and TPU are popular filament materials, each with different properties for various applications.

Slicing Software

Slicers convert 3D models into printing instructions (G-code) that tell the printer how to create each layer.

Design Considerations

Wall thickness, overhangs, support structures, and bed adhesion are critical factors for successful 3D prints.

The 3D Printing Process

Follow these steps to bring your ideas from concept to physical object

1

Design

Create a 3D model using CAD software like Tinkercad, Fusion 360, or Blender. Consider the limitations and capabilities of 3D printing during the design phase.

2

Export

Save your design as an STL or OBJ file. These formats contain the surface geometry of your model without color or material information.

3

Slice

Import your STL file into slicing software like Cura or PrusaSlicer. Configure print settings like layer height, infill, and supports, then generate G-code.

4

Print

Transfer the G-code to your 3D printer. Ensure the print bed is level and clean, then start the printing process which can take from minutes to days.

5

Post-Process

Remove the printed object from the build plate, clean up support material, and perform any necessary finishing like sanding, painting, or assembly.
